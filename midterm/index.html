Life of a Sunflower

The sunflower is a bright and cheerful plant known for its large yellow petals and tall green stem. Its scientific name, Helianthus annuus, comes from the Greek words “helios” meaning “sun” and “anthos” meaning “ flower,” because the sunflower turns its face toward the sun as it moves across the sky. Native to North America, sunflowers are now grown all over the world for their beauty and usefulness. They are an important source of food and oil, as their seeds are rich in nutrients and used to produce healthy sunflower oil for cooking. Sunflowers also play a key role in nature by attracting bees and other pollinators, helping the environment stay balanced. Beyond their practical value, sunflowers symbolize happiness, possitivity, and loyalty. Their bright, sun-like appearance brings warmth and joy wherever they grow, making them one of the most admired flowers in the world.

Facts About Sunflowers

  • Sunflowers can grow very tall- some reach over 3 meters(about 10 feet).
  • Sunflowers follow the sun only when young.
  • There are more than 70 species of sunflowers.
  • Sunflower seeds come in two types. Black seeds use for oil production, while striped seeds are use as a snack.
